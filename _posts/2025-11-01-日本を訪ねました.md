# 日本語の勉強

Since this trip started to get planned about a year in advance, I decided to try studying some Japanese. After a year, I felt somewhat confident navigating around metro stations, reading menus and signs, and speaking with employees at restaurants and stores. I _definitely_ wished I had practiced output at least a little, but しょうがない ¯\\\_(ツ)_/¯.

My method is far from unique, but I figured I would write down the resources I used anyway.

## [Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net/)

Anki is a (free and open-source 😎) spaced repetition program (think fancy flashcards that know when to show you a card again based on how well you tell it you remembered a card). I used two different "core" decks (listed below) that are supposed to have the most commonly used words in a language, and took 10 to 20 new cards a day. My reviews lasted anywhere from 15 minutes to 1 hour depending on whether or not I decided to listen to the example sentence audio that day.

- Kaishi 1.5k
- Core 2k/6k

## Grammar

There's a lot of _d i s c o u r s e_ online about the best way to learn grammar in a new language (textbooks, exercises, immersion, etc.) so I tried to do a bit of everything. Here's a list of the main grammar resources I used (in no particular order):

- [Cure Dolly](https://www.youtube.com/@organicjapanesewithcuredol49) (videos discussing grammar from the ground up)
