Welcome to my very professional and very in-depth thoughts on the books I read in 2026.

**住野よるの『また、同じ夢を見ていた』**: これは日本語で小説を読むこと初めてでしたが、どんな面白くなかった本でした。でも、たくさん新しい言葉とフレーズを見たし日本語の生徒に勧めるのは理解出来ました。3/5.

***The Fisherman* by John Langan**: I really liked the pacing of this book. It always felt like it was moving forward and gave just enough details to flesh out the story without getting bogged down. Or maybe I just feel that way because the last book I read was in a language I don't speak and it took me longer to get through it than it probably should have. What I do know for sure is that I quite liked how the idea of a world like ours (but not exactly) existing underneath us mirrored the story within a story structure, and how that was also reflected in the physical description of the creek and its surroundings. 4/5.

***The Picture of Dorian Gray* by Oscar Wilde**: Guess you really gotta watch out for those Lord Henry types, huh? Honestly, I don't understand how any of these characters could stand listening to this guy. He would say so little of consequence despite spewing so many words, and what little he did say, he would often contradict (sometimes later in the same conversation!). Quite insufferable, which is not to say that all the other characters seemed pleasant to be around (looking at you, Sir Geoffrey). Anyway, at least the story and Dorian's internal struggle were interesting. 3.5/5.

**川村元気の『8番出口』**: そう、映画に基づいている本です。だが、いくつか新たな異変と説明的場面ありました。なお、登場人物の異なる話し方を読み比べるのは面白かったです。読むのにたった一週間しかかからなかったということは、私は本当に日本語上手になっていますかな。4.5/5.

***The Martian* by Andy Weir**: *Project Hail Who*? I assume most people who are choosing to read a Weir novel at this point in time are reading the one that inspired the big record-breaking movie that premiered this year, but I preferred *The Martian*. Despite being a certified sci-fi hater, I had a pretty good time with this novel. My only real complaint was the repetitiveness of Watney's plot on Mars at points. There's only so many times I can read through the cycle of "Here's a crazy science problem, but I'm a super science genius, so here's how I'm going to solve it. Oh no, there's another crazy science problem!" before I get a bit tired. The scenes detailing the work of the scientists on Earth and the other astronauts on the *Hermes* were much more compelling in my opinion. Fortunately, Watney's brand of humor was enough to keep all the Mars stuff mostly interesting as well. 4/5.

***The Turn of the Screw* Henry James**: What a dreadfully uninteresting story. 1/5.
